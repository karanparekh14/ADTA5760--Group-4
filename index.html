Overview

About the Project

HW2 for ADTA 5770 involves constructing two domain-specific knowledge bases and deliberately contaminating each PDF document with by-design errors to study the impact on downstream Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) pipelines.

üì¶

Supply Chain Knowledge Base

100 PDF documents covering supply chain management, logistics, procurement, inventory optimization, and risk management. Each document was contaminated with 3 by-design errors targeting domain-specific terminology.

üè•

Medical Knowledge Base

50 PDF documents spanning clinical research, medical literature, pharmacology, and healthcare management. Contaminations were carefully placed to introduce plausible but incorrect medical information.

üéØ

Project Objectives

  • Collect 150 domain-specific PDF documents across supply chain and medical fields
  • Apply 3 types of by-design contamination per document: Typo, Conflicting, and Nonsense
  • Use in-place PDF text replacement to contaminate at randomly selected locations
  • Document every contaminant with ID, type, original text, replacement, and exact location
  • Study how contaminated knowledge bases affect RAG pipeline answer quality

Contamination Types

Three distinct contamination strategies were applied across both knowledge bases, each targeting a different type of error that could mislead a RAG system.

Typo Contamination

Deliberate misspellings of domain-specific technical terms ‚Äî subtle enough to appear like human error, yet capable of disrupting keyword-based retrieval and confusing language models during answer generation.

Conflicting Information

Substitution of accurate domain terms with plausible but factually incorrect alternatives ‚Äî for example replacing a correct technical concept with a contradictory one. These are the most dangerous contaminants for RAG pipelines.

Count 147 injected
Examples effective ‚Üí ineffective
Impact Factual errors in answers

Approach

  • ~40 antonym/conflicting pairs across both knowledge base domains
  • Targets high-signal words (increase/decrease, effective/ineffective, etc.)
  • Applied on a different page than the typo contamination per document
  • Designed to produce confidently wrong RAG responses

Nonsense / Absurd Replacement

Replacement of meaningful domain terms with completely unrelated, absurd phrases ‚Äî such as replacing “supply chain” with “dolphin choir”. These are the most detectable but test whether RAG systems flag incoherent passages.

Pipeline

Contamination Pipeline

End-to-end automated pipeline for building and contaminating domain-specific knowledge bases.

01

Document Collection

Curated 150 PDF documents: 100 supply chain (logistics, procurement, inventory, risk) and 50 medical (clinical, pharmacology, healthcare management).

02

Contamination Mapping

Built domain-specific word maps: 50+ typo pairs, ~40 conflicting pairs, ~35 nonsense pairs ‚Äî covering terminology from both supply chain and medical domains.

03

Font Extraction & Matching

Each PDF's existing fonts are extracted using PyMuPDF. Replacement text is rendered using the exact same font, size, and position as the original text.

04

In-Place PDF Replacement

Original text is redacted using a white rectangle overlay, then the replacement text is drawn in-place with PyMuPDF TextWriter ‚Äî visually seamless, no annotations.

05

Documentation & Verification

Every contaminant logged with document ID, contaminant ID, type, original text, replacement, context snippet, and exact page coordinates. Exported as TXT, CSV, and Excel.

Key Findings

1

Multi-word Phrases Are Tricky

Phrases like “supply chain” spanning line breaks in PDFs require per-word rect validation ‚Äî naive redaction creates overlapping text artifacts.

2

Font Extraction is Key

84% of replacements used the document's own embedded fonts via PyMuPDF, ensuring the contaminated text is visually indistinguishable from the original.

3

Conflicting Errors Are Most Dangerous

Substitutions like “effective” ‚Üí “ineffective” are the hardest to detect and most likely to cause RAG systems to generate confidently wrong answers.

4

Automated Documentation

All 443 contaminants are fully documented with exact page coordinates, context snippets, and contaminant IDs ‚Äî exported as TXT, CSV, and Excel.
